# Koldarc — Brand Brief

_Source of truth for the Koldarc identity. Everything we design (logo, site,
decals, print) should trace back to this._

---

## 1. Snapshot

- **Name:** Koldarc
- **What:** Mobile welding & metal fabrication — we bring the rig to you.
- **Where:** Knoxville & East Tennessee (farms, ranches, rural property first).
- **Relationship to KoldOps:** **Standalone identity** (its own mark and name),
  but shares the KoldOps **cool color family** — kin by palette, not a sub-brand.

## 2. Positioning

Koldarc is the welder that runs like a real business. We do the gritty,
hands-on work of the trade — on a cracked frame in a muddy field — but we quote
clearly, show up insured and on time, and stand behind the weld. **Blue-collar
hands, white-collar standards.**

## 3. Audience

- **Primary:** Farms & ranches — equipment, gates, trailers, on-site repair on
  rural property. Values toughness, speed, and someone who'll actually come out.
- **Secondary:** Contractors, rural homeowners, fleets needing on-site repair.

## 4. Personality

| We are…                | We are not…              |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Hard-working, hands-on | Corporate, sterile       |
| Dependable, accountable| Fly-by-night, "cash only"|
| Plain-spoken           | Slick or salesy          |
| Proud of the craft     | Sloppy / "good enough"   |
| Run like a business    | Disorganized             |

Two words: **rugged + accountable.**

## 5. The Big Idea — "The Kold Brand"

A branding iron is always **hot**. Koldarc flips it: a **cold brand.**

The logo is a **forged cattle-brand monogram** — the kind of mark you'd see
burned into a ranch gate or a steer's hide — but rendered **cold**: frosted
steel and cool blue instead of glowing red-hot iron.

Why it works on every level:

- **Welding** forges and joins steel — the mark looks forged from bar stock.
- **Ranching** brands livestock — the glyph reads as a registered cattle brand.
- **Ownership** — a brand _is_ ownership. That's the white-collar mentality.
- **The name** — "Kold" + "arc." A cold brand is the literal pun made visual.

Tagline territory: _"The Kold Brand."_ / _"Branded cold. Built to hold."_

## 6. The Mark

- **Form:** A **forged brand-iron monogram** built around **K** (optionally a
  K+A ligature), drawn in the visual grammar of real cattle brands — thick,
  simple, single-weight strokes that could be bent from one bar of steel.
- **Cattle-brand styling:** lean on real brand vocabulary — e.g. a "Rocking K"
  (K on a curved rocker), "Bar K" (K with a bar), or "Lazy K" (tilted). The
  rocker/bar curve can double as a subtle **welding arc**.
- **Cold cues:** frosted/brushed-steel surface, a faint cool-blue rim-light or
  frost, maybe a hairline of frost on the lower edge — never a red/orange glow.
- **Construction:** reads as one continuous forged bar where possible — welds/
  joins can be expressed as small nodes where strokes meet.

## 7. Color — cool / KoldOps family

Cool, icy, Cézanne-blue palette (confirm exact values against the official
KoldOps guide if one exists):

| Role            | Hex       | Use                                   |
| --------------- | --------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Charcoal base   | `#0F1620` | Backgrounds                           |
| Steel           | `#34536B` | The forged metal of the mark          |
| Cézanne blue    | `#4A7A9B` | Primary brand blue                    |
| Ice / frost     | `#79D2F2` | Cold accent, rim-light, highlights    |
| Frost white     | `#EAF6FB` | Type on dark, highlights              |
| Slate (muted)   | `#5B7488` | Secondary text                        |

**No molten orange.** Heat is the thing we deliberately removed.

## 8. Typography (direction)

- **Wordmark:** a sturdy **slab serif** or heavy **condensed grotesque** — it
  should feel stamped/forged and rural-tough, not delicate. Slab serifs read
  "heritage ag"; condensed reads "industrial." Lean slab for warmth, condensed
  for grit.
- **All-caps**, tight tracking, heavy weight. "KOLDARC" should look stamped
  into steel.

## 9. Where it lives

Gates & equipment (the brand burned/plated on), truck doors, hats & hi-vis,
yard signs, website, invoices & quotes. **Must work in one color** (a single
steel/blue or knocked-out white) for plates, stencils, and stamps — like a real
brand that's pressed in one hit.

## 10. Do / Don't

- ✅ One forged mark, simple enough to stamp or cut from plate.
- ✅ Cold palette; frosted metal; calm confidence.
- ✅ Cattle-brand authenticity (real brand conventions).
- ❌ No hot/red/orange glow, no flames, no generic lightning bolt.
- ❌ No clip-art welding helmets or sparks-everywhere clichés.
- ❌ Nothing that looks like a tech startup or the KoldOps logo.

## 11. Generation direction (for the Gemini image pipeline)

Prompt essence to iterate on:

> A forged steel cattle-brand monogram of the letter K, in the style of a real
> livestock branding iron, built from a single thick bar of welded steel.
> Rendered **cold** — frosted brushed steel with a cool blue frost rim-light, on
> a deep charcoal background. Simple, bold, single-weight, stampable. A subtle
> curved rocker/arc under the K. Icy KoldOps palette (steel, Cézanne blue, ice
> blue, frost white). No heat, no orange, no flames. Minimal, ownable brand mark.

Variations to explore: Rocking K, Bar K, Lazy K, K+A ligature; brand-on-metal
plate vs. knockout; with/without the arc rocker.
